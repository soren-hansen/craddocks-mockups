Compliance Context

What Joint Commission EOC Surveyors Look For

Joint Commission Environment of Care standards require documented evidence that non-critical patient care equipment is cleaned and maintained. Here's how Craddock's documentation maps to those requirements.

What EOC Surveyors Expect

  • Evidence that non-critical equipment is included in the cleaning program
  • Documentation of products used — EPA-registered, appropriate for the surface
  • Frequency of cleaning — consistent with the facility's infection control policy
  • Responsibility assigned — who performs the cleaning and supervises it
  • Condition records — evidence that equipment is maintained in working, safe condition

What Craddock's Provides

  • Cleaning log naming every unit cleaned, with date and supervisor sign-off
  • EPA product records: registration number, dilution rate, application method
  • Scheduled program — documented frequency, not ad hoc
  • Named supervisor accountable for every cleaning session
  • Before/after photographic record of equipment condition
⚖️

Important: Craddock's provides cleaning services and documentation support. We are not a compliance consultancy and do not provide legal or regulatory advice. Facilities should work with their infection control and compliance teams to confirm documentation requirements for their specific accreditation program.

Common Questions

What EVS Managers Ask Before They Book

What types of medical equipment does Craddock's clean?+
We clean non-critical patient care equipment including wheelchairs, transport chairs, stretchers and gurneys, IV poles, medication carts, mobile workstations (WOWs), shower chairs, and commodes. All work includes rust treatment and caster/wheel restoration where applicable.
Is the documentation Joint Commission EOC compliant?+
Our documentation is structured to support Joint Commission Environment of Care standards for non-critical patient care equipment. After every job you receive before/after photos, a timestamped unit-level cleaning log, supervisor sign-off, and EPA product records. Your compliance team should confirm the documentation meets your specific accreditation requirements.
How do you schedule around active patient care?+
We work by floor and department, coordinating with your EVS team around patient care schedules. Equipment is cleaned in batches during off-peak hours or when units are not in active use. Completed units are returned to service promptly with documentation.
What products do you use and are they safe for medical equipment surfaces?+
We use EPA-registered products selected for compatibility with each equipment type. Product records including EPA registration number, dilution rate, and application method are included in the cleaning log delivered after every job. We carry product information for review before any work starts.
Can you set up a recurring program rather than one-off visits?+
Yes. Most facilities benefit from a scheduled quarterly or monthly program rather than reactive cleaning. We agree a frequency and scope in writing, build it into your facility's CleanProof System documentation schedule, and deliver the same log format after every session. No surprises, no renegotiation each time.

How We Operate

The 5-Step Accountability Protocol — Every Visit, Every Facility

This isn't a marketing promise. It's the operational sequence our supervisors run on every job. If any step isn't completed, the shift isn't signed off.

1

Scope Confirmed

No assumptions. A written, facility-specific cleaning checklist is reviewed and confirmed before any crew sets foot in your building.

2

Crew Briefed

Every team member reviews the facility checklist at the start of every shift. New areas, special instructions, compliance requirements — all covered before work starts.

3

Supervisor Inspection

Not a spot check — a full checklist walkthrough. The supervisor signs off on every area before the crew leaves. Every area checked against the facility checklist.

4

Cleaning Log Delivered

A timestamped digital log — completed checklist, supervisor sign-off, EPA product records, compliance notes — is emailed to the facility manager before the next business day.

5

Feedback Loop Closed

You have a named point of contact and a direct line to the team. Not a call center, not a ticket queue. Issues are resolved same-shift and documented before the log is sent.

📊

Result: You have a paper trail for every visit — ready for Joint Commission surveys, OSHA inspections, or your own internal audits. No chasing. No guessing.

Common Questions

Questions Facility Managers Ask Before Signing

How does Craddock's document each cleaning visit? +
After every visit, we email you a timestamped digital cleaning log. It includes the completed facility checklist, supervisor sign-off, EPA-registered product records, and any compliance notes. You receive it before your next business day starts — no need to ask.
What is the 5-Step Accountability Protocol? +
It's the operational sequence we run on every job: (1) Scope confirmed in writing; (2) Crew briefed on the facility checklist every shift; (3) Supervisor inspection before leaving; (4) Digital log emailed to you; (5) Feedback loop closed — your dedicated point of contact handles any follow-up same day.
Do you clean medical facilities? Is your documentation Joint Commission compliant? +
Yes. We clean hospitals, outpatient clinics, and medical office buildings across DFW. Our documentation includes EPA-registered product records, before/after photographic documentation for medical equipment cleaning, and EOC-compliant logs that satisfy Joint Commission Environment of Care standards.
What happens when a crew member calls in sick? +
Sick-outs are our problem, not yours. We maintain a backup crew system. If a scheduled team member is unavailable, a qualified replacement is deployed. Your facility is cleaned on schedule — you won't know anything was different unless we tell you.
Are there lock-in contracts or upcharges? +
No lock-in contracts. No hidden upcharges. Scope and pricing are agreed in writing before we start. If your needs change, we adjust the scope — you get an updated proposal, not a surprise invoice.
